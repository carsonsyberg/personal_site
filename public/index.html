Electric Vehicle Ecosystem Exhibit

This past spring, after a year of planning and building, my capstone team "Grid vs Evil" won the 2022 Sustainability Solutions Showcase in the energy category, as well as the judges top score out of 14 electrical engineering teams at the 2022 CU Engineering Expo. Our project was an educational exhibit designed to teach users about electric vehicles being used for energy transport and storage. We built a model city across three 4 foot by 8 foot tables. LED strips were laid out across the tables to simulate the flow of energy around an electrical grid, to simulate charging and discharging of the vehicle, and to display a power outage scenario to highlight how electric cars could improve grid resiliency. Driving through our model city was a model electric vehicle, complete with a 3D printed Tesla body, a custom PCB, and a navigation algorithm to allow recalculating paths around the city to certain destinations. The third part of our exhibit was a tablet mounted on the center table with a progressive web app that communicated to the model via bluetooth. On the web app were location choices to send the car to, as well as educational information describing the scenario they see on the model. We originally built this project in the basement of our engineering center, so ensuring it was easy to take apart and put back together was crucial. In total, we had to present our exhibit at 3 different locations. The exhibit separated into 6 tables, the buildings have pegs at the corners to slot into the tables, the legs of the tables come off, and the LED strips on the tables disconnect from each other with pin connectors under the table connections.
